Overview

This was a volunteer project for a local start-up called IceBrekr in Nashville. Icebrekr is a networking application, like a mix of LinkedIn and Instagram. The company came to Nashville Entrepreneur Center with their observations from beta testing.

The Problem

Beta test users often stopped the sign-up process very early on, and those who complete the sign-up process fail to edit the profiles or show any further activity on the app.

Root Cause

The beta testing showed that users were put off by the complicated sign-in process and confusion about the app's purpose once they logged in, leaving users unsure about how to answer certain questions and complete their profile.

Asking the Right Questions

What is IceBrekrs Goal?

By identifying a clear goal I can determine the questions I need to ask to extract the best insights from the qualitative data IceBrekr provided.
The goal of this redesign is to create a user jounrey that is inuitive and easy naviage, therefore increasing the number of users that have signed in an completed creating their account.

So, if users aren't completing the Sign-in/Profile process, where are they stopping?

Users often stopped when interacting with LinkedIn's login button and the "Fill From LinkedIn Profile" button.
Additionally, when it came to the profile page many users stopped editing their profile after adding a picture.

The red circles indicate the most frequent final touchpoint of each page before the user quits.

Why are users stopping at these prompts?

To put it simply, the whole process is too confusing. Even the slightest deferral of attention or unclear step that gets in the way can intimidate users into giving up or getting distracted.

The cognitive load cost of these roadblocks outweighs the personal importance of yet another social media app.

"I keep getting sent back to the original login page after using the LinkedIn button but none of my information was showing up. I kind of just gave up after that, like thats just way too much work."

"After I got to the word picking part I was so confused because like I don't even know what this app is for. How am I supposed to know what words to pick?"

Tools to Create a Solution

Each UX case has different methodology needs. Thats the importance of data analysis skills in UX research. By knowing what I'm looking for I can determine which methodologies best fit my goal.

Usability Results

These results were provided by IceBrekr, now they just need to be analyzed. This data provides user's ability to complete a goal and helps us answer why users are stopping.

User Personas

I need to get a firm grasp on who would be using the app. This can tell us which design formatting will be most recognizable and usable in the apps context.

Journey Mapping

Lastly, I need to create an ideal user journey. In doing so I can complete a gap analysis, which will help me compare the current state of the app to IceBrekr's goals to pinpoint exact changes.
